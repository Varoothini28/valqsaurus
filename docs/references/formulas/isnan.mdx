title: ISNAN
---
The ISNAN function returns a default value if the value in the node is an error.

## Syntax
ISNAN (value1,value2)

## Arguments
value1 – The node that is to be checked for error values

value2 – The value which is to replace the error values


## Example
Consider the following nodes: Cost per unit has divide by zero error values and it has to be updated to 0.

| Period        | Jan | Feb | Mar | Apr | May | Jun | Jul | Aug | Sep | Oct | Nov | Dec |
|---------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Total Cost    | 100 | 150 | 200 | 100 | 300 | 250 | 300 | 0   | 225 | 300 | 0   | 300 |
| No of units   | 10  | 15  | 10  | 10  | 15  | 25  | 15  | 0   | 15  | 15  | 0   | 15  |
| Cost per unit | 10  | 10  | 20  | 10  | 20  | 10  | 20  | #   | 15  | 20  | #   | 20  |

This can be achieved by using the formula
ISNAN(Cost per unit,0)
